# Quill v1.1.13

**Claude can work with line breaks now.**

Building on 1.1.12 (which made editing _across_ a line break safe), Claude can now **create and remove** hard line breaks — the Shift+Enter kind you use inside an address, a signature, or a poem.

- **Join two lines or split one.** Ask Claude to "put each item on its own line" or "join these two lines," and it proposes the change as a tracked suggestion, just like any other edit. It stays within one paragraph — joining or splitting separate paragraphs still needs Editing mode, and Claude will say so rather than doing something unexpected.
- **Line breaks are visible in review.** A pending line-break change shows a **↵** marker at the exact spot in the document (green for added, red for removed), and the review card reads **"↵ line break"** instead of a blank — so you always see what you're accepting.

No file-format or data changes. Documents, comments, suggestions, chat threads, and linked sessions carry over unchanged.

## Install

Inside Truss, Quill is shared as a ready-to-run app on the team's Google Drive — copy `Quill.app` from the shared drive (in Finder, from your synced folder) to your Applications folder and open it. The [README](../../README.md#installing-quill) has the details and the [User Guide](../USER_GUIDE.md) a full walkthrough. The Claude features require the [Claude Code](https://claude.com/claude-code) CLI on the same Mac.

## Known limitations

- macOS only. Not yet Apple-notarized — a copy downloaded through a browser (e.g. the Drive website) is typically quarantined and trips Gatekeeper on first launch; open the Finder-synced copy instead, or use **System Settings → Privacy & Security → Open Anyway**.
- Single-user: multiple documents open as tabs in one window; no multi-user collaboration or cloud sync.
- Suggesting mode still cannot merge, split, or restructure whole paragraphs, list items, or other blocks as a tracked change — Quill tells you when a change needs Editing mode.
